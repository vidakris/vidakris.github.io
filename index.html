About me

I'm astronomer at the Konkoly Observatory. My main research field is studying stellar activity: a group of phenomena strongly connected to the magnetic fields of stars. These can be linked to starspots, activity cycles and transient events, like flares and coronal mass ejections (CMEs) that can have serious consequences on the neighborhood of the stars.

I'm interested in observing these events - either using ground-based telescopes or space observatories, like the Kepler, TESS or PLATO, and using machine learning to effectively process the vast amount of data available to researchers. I am also involved in the Fly's Eye project, and ESA's ARIEL and PLATO space missions, helping mainly in the Stellar activity and Fast photometry; and flare detection working groups.
